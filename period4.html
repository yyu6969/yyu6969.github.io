U.S. Economic Growth: A Detailed Analysis of GDP from 2018-2022

The graph shows the U.S. GDP from 2018 to 2022, highlighting a significant downturn in 2020 followed by a recovery. Initially, the GDP continues its upward trajectory until 2020, when it faces a sharp decline, attributed to the economic repercussions of the COVID-19 pandemic. This downturn is marked by disruptions in supply chains, reduced consumer and business activity due to necessary public health measures, and widespread business closures or scale-backs, all contributing to a surge in unemployment and a decrease in economic output. The graph then shows a recovery phase beginning in 2021, as interventions to stabilize the economy likely took effect, demonstrating the dynamic nature of economic health in response to global crises.
